How it works

Press. Speak. Paste.

No windows to open, no modes to enter. Blurt lives on a single key and types into whatever already has focus.

  1. 01

    Tap or hold right ⌘

    One lone modifier key is the whole interface. Tap to toggle, hold for push-to-talk — and it's rebindable in Settings.

  2. 02

    Say the thing

    A synth cue confirms recording. Speak naturally — a sentence or a whole rambling paragraph, in whatever language you think in.

  3. 03

    Polished text appears

    AssemblyAI transcribes and cleans up your speech in a single pass, and Blurt pastes the result right where your cursor is.

Why Blurt

Small app, sharp edges.

Works anywhere you can type

Mail, Slack, your editor, a browser text box — Blurt pastes the transcript into the focused Mac app, whatever it is.

Polished in one step

Audio goes to AssemblyAI's Sync STT API and comes back as clean text. No model downloads, no separate cleanup pass, no waiting on a second model.

Context-aware

Blurt primes each request with the app, window, and text around your cursor, plus your own key terms — so names and jargon come back spelled right.

Small native app

The setup window, overlay, hotkey, and paste flow are built for macOS with AppKit and SwiftUI. No Electron, no daemon farm.

Actual synth cues

Start and stop can be cued by real Yamaha DX7 or Roland Juno-106 sounds — or turned off, if you'd rather blurt in silence.

Open source, MIT

The whole app — engine, UI, prompt — is on GitHub. Read it, build it, fork it.

Privacy

Your words stay yours.

  • No stored audio, no stored transcripts. Audio is captured only while you dictate, sent over HTTPS to AssemblyAI, and never kept by Blurt.
  • Your key, your account. The AssemblyAI API key lives in the macOS Keychain — transcription runs against your own account, under AssemblyAI's privacy policy.
  • Diagnostics only. Release builds send crash reports and anonymous install events to Datadog — never audio, transcripts, or your API key.
